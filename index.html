Privacy Policy for Crypto Mining Empire

Last updated: July 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Crypto Mining Empire (package name: com.dennis.cryptominingempire) collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the game.

This policy is written for the Android version of the game. iOS support is not part of the current release setup.

Developer / data controller: Dennis Reimer
Contact: Affirmion@gmail.com

1. Summary

Crypto Mining Empire is an idle/clicker game. The game may process limited data to:

  • save your game progress;
  • provide optional cloud save and account linking;
  • show rewarded and interstitial ads;
  • request and manage advertising consent and privacy choices where required;
  • process in-app purchases when enabled;
  • provide daily rewards, offline production, timers, achievements, prestige systems, and other gameplay features;
  • prevent fraud, debug problems, improve stability, and comply with platform requirements.

The game does not intentionally collect your precise location, contacts, photos, microphone recordings, camera data, SMS, health data, or user-generated chat content.

2. Information stored locally on your device

The game stores gameplay data locally using Unity PlayerPrefs or equivalent local storage. This can include:

  • coin balance, cash balance, quantum balance, and similar in-game currency values;
  • machine levels, upgrades, purchases, prestige progress, tutorial state, achievements, daily rewards, offline production timestamps, active boosts, bubble states, settings, audio preferences, selected scene, and similar gameplay progress;
  • privacy-policy acceptance state;
  • local snapshots of your save data for backup/restore comparison;
  • purchase-related local status when in-app purchases are enabled.

This local data usually stays on your device unless you use cloud save/account linking, your operating system or store platform backs it up, or you manually provide logs/screenshots for support.

3. Unity services

The game uses Unity-related services for functionality such as authentication, cloud save, economy features, and game development/runtime technology.

Depending on your actions and the final release configuration, Unity services may process anonymous player identifiers, linked account identifiers, cloud save data, economy/currency balances, inventory/resource data, device/app/diagnostic/crash/performance information, IP address, and network information needed to provide the service.

Unity’s own privacy information is available here: https://unity.com/legal/privacy-policy

4. Google Play Games sign-in

The game supports Google Play Games sign-in on Android for account linking and cloud-save continuity.

If you choose to sign in with Google Play Games, Google and Unity Authentication may process your Google Play Games player/account identifier, authentication tokens or server auth codes, account-linking status, basic profile/account data made available by Google Play Games, and technical data required for authentication and fraud prevention.

The game does not intentionally use Google Play Games sign-in to access your contacts, emails, photos, or unrelated Google account data.

Google’s privacy policy is available here: https://policies.google.com/privacy

5. Cloud save and save comparison

When cloud save is active, the game can upload and download save snapshots. A save snapshot may include balances, in-game currencies, machines, upgrades, prestige progress, unlocks, achievements, tutorial progress, daily reward state, offline production timestamps, timers, boost states, store/economy gameplay data, and cloud/local comparison values.

When a cloud save is found, the game may compare local and cloud progress and ask which save you want to use.

6. Advertising

The game uses Google AdMob / Google Mobile Ads to show ads, including rewarded ads and interstitial ads.

Rewarded ads may be used to grant optional benefits such as bonus cash, doubled offline rewards, daily reward bonuses, or temporary boosts. Interstitial ads may be used at natural breaks if enabled.

Google Mobile Ads may automatically collect and share data such as IP address, device and account identifiers including advertising identifiers where available and permitted, app/ad interactions, and diagnostic, crash, and performance information.

Google’s AdMob / Google Mobile Ads data disclosure information is available here: https://developers.google.com/admob/android/privacy/play-data-disclosure

Google AdMob policies are available here: https://support.google.com/admob/answer/6128543

Depending on your region and the final production setup, ads may be personalized or non-personalized. Where legally required, consent should be requested before personalized advertising or storage/access of information on your device for advertising purposes.

The game may use Google’s User Messaging Platform (UMP) to request, store, update, and communicate your advertising consent choices. If privacy options are required in your region, the game may show an ad-consent form and may provide an in-game “Ad Privacy Choices” option so you can review or change your advertising choices.

7. In-app purchases

The game is prepared for in-app purchases through Google Play Billing / Unity IAP. Current or planned purchases may include cash packs or other digital items.

If you make a purchase, Google Play and/or Unity IAP may process product ID, purchase token/order information, transaction status, country/region and payment-related information handled by Google Play, and information needed to verify, restore, or deliver purchases. The developer does not receive your full payment card details from Google Play.

Google Play’s privacy information and terms apply to payments and purchase processing: https://policies.google.com/privacy and https://play.google.com/about/play-terms/

8. Economy and virtual items

The game may use Unity Economy or equivalent backend services to mirror or manage certain virtual resources, balances, inventory items, or purchase states. This may include cash balance, prestige points, virtual purchase state, resource IDs and inventory/resource amounts, timestamps, and technical records needed to sync or validate data.

9. Online time, daily rewards, and offline production

The game uses time information to calculate daily rewards, offline production, boost timers, and similar features.

The game may request a current UTC time from an online time endpoint such as worldtimeapi.org or a future replacement service. This request is used to reduce abuse of device-clock changes and to calculate time-based rewards. The game does not intentionally send your save data to that time service, but the service may receive technical network information such as your IP address as part of normal internet communication.

10. Analytics, diagnostics, and crash/performance data

The game may process diagnostic or performance information through Unity, Google Play, Google Mobile Ads, or platform tools. This may include app version, device model, operating system version, language/region, technical identifiers, crash logs, error logs, stack traces, performance data, ad-load diagnostics, and authentication/cloud-save/purchase/service error logs.

11. Data sharing

We may share or make data available to Unity Technologies and Unity service providers; Google, including Google Play, Google Play Games, Google Mobile Ads / AdMob, and Google Play Billing; time service providers such as WorldTimeAPI or a future equivalent; hosting providers for the privacy-policy website, such as GitHub Pages if used; and legal/regulatory authorities if required by law.

We do not sell your personal information in the traditional sense. Advertising services may process identifiers and ad-related data for advertising and measurement purposes, depending on your consent, settings, and applicable law.

12. Legal bases for processing

If you are in the EU/EEA, UK, or a similar region, the legal bases may include performance of a contract, consent, legitimate interests, and legal obligation.

13. Retention

Local save data remains on your device until you delete it, reset progress, clear app data, or uninstall the game. Cloud save, economy, authentication, purchase, advertising, diagnostic, and platform records may be retained by Unity, Google, or other service providers according to their own policies and as needed to provide the service, resolve disputes, prevent fraud, comply with legal obligations, or maintain security.

14. Your choices and rights

Depending on your region, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or port your personal data, withdraw consent, or complain to a data protection authority.

You can also avoid linking a Google Play Games account, clear local app data, reset gameplay progress where available, review or change advertising consent choices through the in-game ad privacy options where available, manage ad personalization in Android/Google settings, and manage Google Play Games account settings through Google.

To make a privacy request, contact: Affirmion@gmail.com

15. Children

The game is not intended for children under the age required by applicable law to consent to data processing, and it is not designed as a child-directed app. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children without appropriate consent.

16. International transfers

Service providers such as Unity and Google may process information in countries outside your country of residence. These countries may have different data-protection laws. Where required, appropriate safeguards should apply.

17. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect game data. However, no system is completely secure. You should protect your Google account, device lock, and app-store account credentials.

18. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when the game changes, services are added or removed, legal requirements change, or app-store requirements change. The updated policy will be posted at the privacy-policy URL used by the game and/or store listing.
